Learn the Nelson-Siegel Model

A short tour of the math, the factors, and how to read the curve.

The equation

The Nelson-Siegel functional form expresses the yield y at maturity τ as:

y(τ) = β0 + β1·[(1 - e-τ/λ) / (τ/λ)] + β2·{[(1 - e-τ/λ) / (τ/λ)] - e-τ/λ}

Three economic factors plus a decay parameter capture nearly any plausible curve shape with only four numbers.

Reading the factors

  • β0 · Level - long-run yield. Parallel shifts of the entire curve.
  • β1 · Slope - short-end loading. Negative when curves are upward-sloping.
  • β2 · Curvature - mid-curve hump. Positive = belly above the line through ends.
  • τ · Decay - where curvature peaks (in years).

Curve shapes & signals

Normal

Upward-sloping. Growth-positive regime.

Inverted

Long < short. Classic recession signal.

Humped

Mid-tenor peak. Mid-cycle uncertainty.

Flat

Tight term premium. Late-cycle.

How to use this app

  1. Open Curve Fitter, paste market quotes (or click Load latest snapshot).
  2. Hit Fit Nelson-Siegel. Read off level/slope/curvature/decay.
  3. Switch to Parameter Lab to see how each factor reshapes the curve.
  4. Use Historical Factors for time-series and Treasury vs TIPS for breakevens.
